Here’s a question. You lead a customer service team. You know how to hire, train and develop top talent. You’ve cut costs where necessary and still provide excellent customer experience. You built a team that’s empathetic and efficient.

And now you realize that AI is going to upend it all. AI has a lower cost per call. It can respond with a 0-second SLA. It works 24/7. It won’t leave your company after training. And it can scale infinitely.

But when you try to implement AI in your company, all hell breaks loose. The AI is hallucinating non-existent discounts. Customers are screaming “Get me a human” into their phones. Your team is demotivated. And then you get the bill, and it turns out that your cost per call has gone up.

Sure, AI has to learn and train itself. And the integrations are brittle and highly technical. But you’re not sure it’s worth all the disruption it’s causing.

How do you implement AI without disrupting your own operations?

We’ve been trying to solve this problem for the past 2 years, ever since AI agents became possible with GPT-3.5. We’ve built AI agents for EU banks, global mobility companies and UK’s fastest growing Fintech.

We learned that to really put an AI agent into production, it has to do everything a human can do at the same level as one. And it has to do this out of the box.

That’s why we built a framework that deploys AI into large customer service teams in 3 steps:

  1. Train the AI with top 1% AI trainers who work side-by-side with your team. The AI should be able to pass the assessment that humans do.
  2. Integrate the best-in-class text and voice models deep into your business processes, making sure that anything a human can do in this job, the AI can, too.
  3. Iterate with you for several months to bring the AI to better-than-human performance that you fully understand and control.

We’re testing the framework with a few select customers with outstanding results. There is a lot to improve, but we think we found the best way to implement AI in a large customer service team today.

You don’t need to upend your whole operations to implement AI.
